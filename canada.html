Should you move to Canada?

Due to a welcoming immigration programs of Canada, a lot of people are wondering whether it is something they should do. I would like to share a framework to make this decision easier.

1. Don’t start by thinking that this is something you cannot undo. In other words, don’t feel stressed thinking that you are going to leave your home country permanently. Have a flexible mindset to return back to your home country if you later on find out that Canada does not offer everything you hoped for.

2. Make sure you have a specific reason to move to Canada. This could be increasing the rate of savings, changing career to another industry, interested in the wester way of living. Having this reason is important as it will help you withstand the struggle that you’ll go through when you relocate.

3. If increasing the rate of savings is the reason for your move, then please do a thorough analysis by projecting your savings for the span of 10 years in your home country vs Canada. Here, the important factors to consider are monthly living expense, one-time yearly expense, average salary increment rate, and taxes. Check salary for your profession on Glassdor and taxes on Neuvoo

If you belong to an industry of profession that pays lower than an industry where there is established corporate culture in your home country then it would mostly make sense to move to Canada Examples of such an industry and profession are healthcare and pharmacist. Example of and industry and a profession that does not fall under this category are IT industry and Software developer.
